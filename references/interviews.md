# Customer Interviews

## Do not pitch. Investigate.

The founder's job in a customer interview is not to convince anyone the idea is good. It's to understand the customer's life as it exists today.

Never ask:

- "Would you use this?"
- "Do you like this idea?"
- "Would you pay for something like this?"

These questions generate encouragement, not truth.

Ask only about the past and the present.

## Start with their reality, not the solution

Good opening questions:

- "Walk me through the last time this happened."
- "What did you do when that came up?"
- "How often does this happen?"
- "What breaks when it happens?"

Looking for concrete stories, not opinions.

If they can't recall a specific recent example, the problem isn't acute.

## Pain without action is not pain

This is the filter most founders skip.

When someone says: "Yeah, that's a big problem for me"

The immediate follow-up is:

- "What are you doing today to deal with it?"
- "What have you tried already?"
- "What does that cost you in time, money, or stress?"

If the answer is "nothing" or "I just deal with it" or "it's annoying but fine" — it's not a real problem. It's a preference.

Real problems already have solutions, even bad ones.

## Measure sacrifice, not excitement

Interest is cheap. Sacrifice is signal.

**Strong signals:**

- They pay for a workaround
- They built a spreadsheet or internal tool
- They changed a workflow to avoid the problem
- They complain unprompted
- They have a budget line item
- They can name competitors without being asked

**Weak signals:**

- Compliments
- Hypotheticals
- "That would be cool"
- "I'd definitely use that"

If the problem matters, it's already costing them something.

## Ask for a next step immediately

Do not end interviews politely. End them with a test.

- "Can I watch you do this next time?"
- "Can you intro me to someone else who has this problem?"
- "Would you try a manual version if I did it for you?"
- "Can I email you when I have something hacked together?"

If they won't take a small step, they won't take a big one later.

## The rule to end on

**People lie about what they want. They don't lie about what they already do.**

Watch behavior. Ignore opinions.

## Interview template

Use this when the founder asks for help preparing for an interview, or when logging a completed one to `~/pre/customers/interviews.md`.

```
START WITH THEIR REALITY
- Walk me through the last time this happened.
- What were you trying to do when it came up?
- What did you do when that happened?
- How did you eventually solve or complete it?
- How often does this happen?
- What breaks when it happens?

WHEN THEY SAY "YEAH, THAT'S A PROBLEM" — DIG DEEPER
- What are you doing today to deal with it?
- What have you tried already?
- What did you search for when looking for a solution?
- Why did you stop using [thing they tried]?
- What does that cost you in time, money, or stress?

IF THEY SAY "NOTHING" OR "I JUST DEAL WITH IT"
→ It's not a real problem. Move on or probe a different angle.

LOOK FOR SACRIFICE (real pain leaves evidence)
- Are you paying for anything to help with this?
- Have you built any workarounds? Spreadsheets? Hacks?
- Have you changed how you do things to avoid this?
- What would happen if you just ignored it completely?

TEST COMMITMENT — DON'T END POLITELY
- Can I watch you do this next time it happens?
- Can you intro me to others who have this problem?
- Would you try a manual version if I did it for you?
- Can I email you when I have something hacked together?

If they won't take a small step, they won't take a big one later.
```

## Logging interviews

Append each completed interview to `~/pre/customers/interviews.md` in this format:

```markdown
## [Date] — [Interviewee first name, role, company type]

**Source:** [how you found them]

**The problem as they described it:**
[direct quotes where possible]

**What they're doing about it today:**
[current solution, workarounds, tools, spreadsheets]

**What it costs them:**
[time, money, stress — in their own words]

**Commitment signal:**
[did they agree to a next step? which one?]

**My takeaway:**
[one or two sentences on what this interview changed about my understanding]
```

The founder should re-read this file every 5 interviews. Patterns emerge on the page that don't emerge in memory.
