# Accountability Partners

Accountability partners are the people who receive weekly progress reports. The right ones make the founder feel genuine pressure to perform. The wrong ones make the report a box to check.

## Who to choose

The best accountability partners are people the founder genuinely doesn't want to disappoint. Not because they'll be mean, but because their opinion matters.

Great choices:

- **Investors** — they already have a stake in the outcome
- **Mentors or advisors** — people whose respect the founder has earned and wants to keep
- **Other founders** — peers who understand the struggle
- **A spouse or partner** — someone who sees the sacrifice and cares about the outcome

Co-founders should be working the same sprint alongside the founder, not receiving reports about it. They see everything directly.

Don't pick people who will just say "nice job" no matter what. Pick people whose silence will sting.

## How many

Start with 2-3. Enough to create real pressure, not so many that the reports feel impersonal.

If the founder has a lot of investors, create an email alias on their domain (like investors@theircompany.com) and use that as a single partner. The weekly update goes to everyone on the list.

## What partners receive

Every week, accountability partners get an automated email showing:

- Weekly goals and their status (done, partial, not done)
- Progress toward the North Star
- A short note from the founder on what worked and what didn't (from the retro)

The founder doesn't have to write much. The data is the report.

If Gmail is connected, the Friday retro scheduled task can draft this email automatically. The founder always reviews and approves before sending.

## The psychology

When the founder knows someone they respect is going to see whether they did what they said they'd do, they're more likely to do it.

This isn't about shame. It's about raising the stakes just enough to overcome the resistance toward hard work.

The best partners don't even need to respond. Just knowing they'll see the update is enough.

## Changing partners

The founder can add or remove partners at any time. If someone isn't creating the right pressure, swap them out.

Some founders change partners between sprints based on what they're working on. An investor for a fundraising sprint. A technical mentor for a product sprint. Fine.

## The report format

The weekly update email should be short. Scannable. No paragraph-long narratives.

```
Subject: [Company] week [N] of [10]

North Star: [goal] — currently at [X] / [target]

This week's goals:
✓ [goal marked done]
~ [goal marked partial — with actual number if applicable]
✗ [goal marked not done]

What worked: [one or two sentences from the retro]
What didn't: [one or two sentences from the retro]
Next week's focus: [what's on deck]

— [Founder name]
```

Keep it this short. Partners are busy. A 6-line email they actually read beats a 3-paragraph update they skim.
